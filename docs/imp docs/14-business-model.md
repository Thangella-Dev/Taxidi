# 14 - Taxiro Business Model

**Date:** 23 July 2026

## Core Business Model

Taxiro can earn through multiple channels, but the first model should be simple and transparent.

## Revenue Streams

| Revenue source | Description | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Commission per ride | Taxiro takes a percentage from each completed ride. | Best first model. |
| Rider subscription | Rider pays daily/weekly/monthly fee to receive leads. | Later, after strong demand exists. |
| Convenience fee | Small fee charged to user on each booking. | Use carefully; may reduce conversion. |
| Surge/peak pricing | Higher fare during high demand. | Use once supply/demand data is reliable. |
| Ads/featured partners | Local businesses promote offers. | Later stage. |
| Corporate/campus plans | Offices/colleges pay for commute support. | Strong B2B opportunity. |
| Delivery/logistics add-on | Use riders during low ride demand. | Later, operationally complex. |

## Recommended Starting Model

Start with:

- **7% platform commission** on completed rides.
- Optional small cancellation fee only when justified.
- No subscription fee at first.
- Use incentives carefully to build supply.

## Why 7% Commission Is Good For Launch

- Lower than many marketplace commissions.
- Rider-friendly.
- Easier to attract supply.
- Allows Taxiro to build trust.
- Can later move to hybrid commission/subscription.

## Money Flow Per Ride

Example ride fare: **Rs 100**

| Item | Amount |
|---|---:|
| User pays | Rs 100 |
| Taxiro commission at 7% | Rs 7 |
| Rider earning before incentives | Rs 93 |
| Payment gateway fee if online payment at approx 2% + GST | About Rs 2.36 |
| Net Taxiro before cloud/support/discounts | About Rs 4.64 |

If user pays cash directly to rider, Taxiro still needs a method to collect commission from rider later through wallet/subscription/settlement. That is why wallet and settlement are important.

## Cash Vs Online Payment

### Cash

Pros:

- Easier for users/riders.
- Lower payment gateway cost.

Cons:

- Harder for Taxiro to collect commission.
- More reconciliation issues.
- Higher fraud/dispute risk.

### Online Payment / Wallet

Pros:

- Taxiro can deduct commission automatically.
- Easier refunds and records.
- Better reporting.

Cons:

- Gateway fees.
- More technical and compliance work.
- Payment failure handling required.

## Best Payment Model For Taxiro

Short term:

- Cash and UPI-after-ride allowed.
- Track commission owed by rider.

Medium term:

- Wallet top-up for users.
- Rider wallet/ledger for commission deductions.

Long term:

- Mostly online payments.
- Automated driver settlement.
- Corporate prepaid wallet.

## Profit Logic

Taxiro becomes profitable when:

Platform commission + other revenue > incentives + payment fees + cloud/API + support + operations + marketing.

The hardest part is not software. It is balancing rider supply, user demand, incentives, and support costs.
